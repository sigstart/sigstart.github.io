Spectrum

What is spectrum?

  • Think of it as capacity
  • It is literally measured in bandwidth, just in different units (Hertz - usually kHz, MHz, or GHz)
  • Frequency is also in Hz, and the two are related

IMT spectrum

(that stands for International Mobile Telecommunications)

  • Standards and recommendations determined by members of the International Telecommunication Union.
  • At meetings like World Radiocommunication Conference, in Geneva.

900MHz band

FDD - gap is called duplex spacing.

1800MHz (1.8GHz) band

1900/2100 (1.9/2.1GHz) band

2600 (2.6GHz) band

Digital Dividend

What's in a G?

When people speak in Gs, they usually mean:

Generation Technology
2G GPRS, EDGE
3G HSPA, HSPA+
4G LTE, LTE-Advanced

Technologies we don't hear about much in South Africa like CDMA2000 and Mobile WiMAX are also considered 3G (and 4G candidates), but not usually marketed that way.

It's all wrong

OK maybe not all, but a lot.

If everyone cared about being technically accurate (according to ITU definitions):

  • EDGE would be referred to as a 3G standard.
  • LTE would be referred to as a 3G standard.
